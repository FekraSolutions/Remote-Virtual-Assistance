
# Posting Entries to the General Ledger: A Step-by-Step Guide

The process of posting accounting entries to the General Ledger is one of the most critical steps in the accounting cycle. After recording financial transactions in the Journal, the posting stage organizes these entries into separate accounts, which helps track balances accurately and understand the company's financial position.



In this article, we will learn how to post accounting entries step-by-step through a simple practical example that illustrates the impact of each operation on different accounts.

---

# What is the Accounting Cycle?

When learning financial accounting, focus is usually placed on four main pillars:

* Basic Accounting Principles

In previous lessons, we covered the concept of a financial event and how to record journal entries. In this lesson, we move to the next stage: **posting these entries to the accounts in the General Ledger**.

---

# What is the General Ledger?

The **General Ledger** is an accounting record containing all of a company's accounts, such as:

* Cash Account
* Capital Account
* Inventory Account
* Accounts Receivable (Customers)
* Accounts Payable (Suppliers)
* Revenue and Expense Accounts

Each account records its movements in a **Debit and Credit format, showing the balance after each transaction**.



---

# Account Format in the General Ledger

An account typically consists of several main columns:

* Movement/Transaction Number
* Description (Particulars)
* Debit
* Credit
* Balance

In older accounting systems, the balance was calculated only at the end of the period, but in modern systems, the balance is shown after every transaction to facilitate tracking and auditing.

# Important Rules for Accounting Accounts

There are basic rules to remember when posting:

* **Asset** accounts are naturally **Debit**.
* **Liability** and **Owner’s Equity** accounts are naturally **Credit**.
* **Expense** accounts are naturally **Debit**.
* **Revenue** accounts are naturally **Credit**.

Understanding these rules makes the process of recording and posting entries much easier.
